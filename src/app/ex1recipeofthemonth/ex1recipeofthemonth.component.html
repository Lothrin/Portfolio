Difficulty: Very Easy
Rating: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★ (749)
  • 6 cups of bulgur (meatball bulgur)
  • 5 cups of hot water
  • 1 tablespoon Salça(heaped pepper paste)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • Tomato (summer tomatoes can be added by chopping them into small pieces)
  • 1 bunch of parsley
  • 6 sprigs of green onion
  • 6-7 leaves of fresh mint
  • 7-8 leaves of lettuce
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon cumin (optional)
  • juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/2 cup of pomegranate syrup
  • 1/2 cup of olive oil
  1. Place 6 tea glasses of bulgur into a deep bowl.
  2. Add 1 heaping tablespoon of pepper paste and mix it into the bulgur with the back of the spoon.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of tomato paste and mix in the same way.
  4. Pour about 5 cups of hot water on it, cover it with a lid and leave it to soften.
  5. Meanwhile, greens are chopped. Chopped parsley, green onion, fresh mint and lettuce are added to the bulghur that has drained and softened.
  6. After mixing the shortbread, add 1 teaspoon of black pepper, 1 teaspoon of salt, half a tea glass of pomegranate syrup, juice of 1 lemon, some cumin if available, and half a tea glass of olive oil and mix well.
  7. It is placed on a sterile serving plate. You can decorate with pickles, cucumbers and tomatoes if desired. Enjoy your meal.

History of Kısır

Kısır dish is a type of salad or appetizer commonly consumed in Middle Eastern and Anatolian cuisine. The basic ingredients of Kisir are bulgur, tomato, cucumber, onion, parsley and lemon juice. In some regions, olives, walnuts or chickpeas are also added to the curd.

Although the origin of the dish is not known for certain, it is thought to have been consumed in Middle Eastern and Anatolian cuisine for centuries. According to some sources, infertility first appeared in Mesopotamia. According to other sources, it emerged in barren Anatolia and later spread to the Middle East.

There are also various rumors about the emergence of sterile food. According to a rumor, it was created by villagers living in barren Anatolia. Villagers would make kefir by mixing ingredients such as bulgur, tomato, cucumber, onion and lemon juice to make a simple and satisfying meal.

Kısır dish has an important place in Middle Eastern and Anatolian cuisine today. It is consumed as a refreshing and satisfying meal, especially in the summer months.

To prepare the meal, bulgur is first washed and drained. Then, tomato, cucumber, onion and parsley are finely chopped. All ingredients are mixed in a bowl and lemon juice is added. Kısır is served by seasoning with salt and black pepper.

Kısır dish can be prepared in different ways. For example, it can be made more satisfying by adding olives, walnuts or chickpeas to the soup. Additionally, yoghurt can be added to the curd.
